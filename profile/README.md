# Mapou Séraphin

**Infectious disease epidemiology · Causal inference · AI/ML for public health**

We apply advanced epidemiologic methods, causal inference frameworks, and machine learning to answer practical questions about infectious disease — and to build tools that translate those answers into public health action.

Our current work uses tuberculosis as a disease model: a pathogen with rich genomic and surveillance data, global relevance, and persistent gaps between what we know and what programs can act on. The methods and tools we develop here are designed to scale.

---

## What we do

Public health decisions are only as good as the evidence behind them. We work at the intersection of rigorous study design, modern statistical methods, and computational tools to generate evidence that is valid, actionable, and scalable.

**Transmission science**
We use whole-genome sequencing, phylodynamic methods, and spatial analysis to reconstruct how pathogens move through populations — identifying transmission networks, evaluating outbreak origins, and characterizing the within-host diversity that complicates between-host inference. The goal is to give TB programs a clearer picture of where transmission is happening and who is driving it.

**Preventive care and treatment**
Eliminating TB in low-incidence settings requires finding and treating latent infection before it progresses. We analyze care cascades, treatment adherence, and risk stratification across health departments and community clinics — applying Bayesian latent class analysis, mixed-effects survival models, and propensity score methods to draw valid causal inferences from routinely collected program data.

**Global health and social determinants**
Disease burden is not randomly distributed. Our global health work — spanning Haiti, Ghana, and the United States — examines how poverty, geography, and social vulnerability shape exposure, diagnosis, and outcomes. We integrate spatial analysis, phylodynamics, and social epidemiology to inform targeted, equity-focused interventions.

**Methods and tools**
We develop and share the analytic pipelines, workflows, and decision-support tools that emerge from this work. Our aim is to make rigorous methods accessible to the public health practitioners who need them most.
